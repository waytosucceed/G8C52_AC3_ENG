Synecdoche is a figure of speech in which a part is used to represent the whole or vice versa, such as using "hands" to refer to workers in the sentence "All hands on deck."

Irony is a rhetorical device where the intended meaning is opposite to the literal meaning, often highlighting a contrast between expectations and reality, like a situational irony when a pilot has a fear of heights.

4: Metaphor üîó
A metaphor is a direct comparison between two unrelated things, suggesting they are alike in a significant way without using "like" or "as," exemplified by the statement "Her voice was music to his ears."
